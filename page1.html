Middlemist's Red Camellia

The Middlemist's Red (Camellia japonica) is considered one of the rarest flowers in the world. It was brought to England from China in 1804 by a botanist named John Middlemist, who lent the flower its name. Only two known examples of the Middlemist's Red exist today: one in the United Kingdom at Chiswick House in London and the other in a private garden in New Zealand.

Despite its name, the flower is a deep pink, rather than true red. It's part of the camellia family, known for their lush, ornamental blooms and glossy evergreen leaves. Camellias generally thrive in temperate climates and require well-drained, acidic soil.

The rarity of the Middlemist's Red is due to over-collection and habitat loss in its native China, but its legacy continues to be preserved in these botanical gardens. The flower has become a symbol of rarity, beauty, and the importance of conservation.
